What to do in Portugal

Templo Romano (Roman Temple)

  • Located on the South side of Portugal.
  • It was built in the 1st century AD and was modified throughout the 2nd and 3rd centuries AD.
  • Some people believe that the Temple was built to honor the roman goddess Diana, but it was actually to honor the Augusto emperor.
  • Largo do Conde de Vila Flor, Évora

Torre de Belém (Belém Tower)

  • This iconic tower was built in 1521.
  • The aim of the fortification was to guard against an invasion via the River Tagus.
  • The building is considered a master class in how to build a successful fortress.
  • It is often portrayed as a symbol of Europe's Age of Discoveries.
  • Since 1983, the tower has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Located in the Belem district in Lisbon.
  • 6 Euros per Adult.

Capela dos Ossos (Chapel of Bones)

  • The “Chapel of Bones” is a small interior chapel located next to the entrance of the Igreja de Sao Francisco (Church of St Francis) that was built in the 16th century by Franciscan friars. It gets its name because the interior walls are covered and decorated with human skulls and bones.
  • The number of skeletons was calculated to be about 5000, coming from the cemeteries that were situated inside several dozen churches.
  • The idea came from one of the Franciscan friars who wanted to prod his fellow brothers into contemplation and transmit the message of life being transitory, a very common spirituality theme summed up in the motto memento mori.
  • At the entrance there is a famous warning: “Nos ossos que aqui estamos pelos vossos esperamos” (“We bones that are here, for yours await”).
  • Praça 1º de Maio, Évora.
  • 5 Euros per Adult.
